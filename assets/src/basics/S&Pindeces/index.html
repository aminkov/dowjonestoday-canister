S&P DJI is a branch of the S&P Global Inc company which manages about 130 000 separate indices... Here are some of the most important ones

Top 15 Dow Jones Indexes Managed by S&P Dow Jones Indices

Introduction

S&P Dow Jones Indices manages a vast array of indices that serve as benchmarks for various segments of the financial markets. Here, we explore the top 15 Dow Jones indexes, which are instrumental in gauging market performance, guiding investment strategies, and providing insights into economic trends.

1. Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA)

The DJIA is the most famous and widely recognized index, comprising 30 large, publicly-owned companies in the U.S. It serves as a key indicator of the overall health of the American economy.

2. Dow Jones Transportation Average (DJTA)

The DJTA includes 20 transportation companies, such as airlines, railroads, and trucking firms. It is considered a leading indicator of economic activity related to the movement of goods and services.

3. Dow Jones Utility Average (DJUA)

This index tracks 15 leading utility companies, including providers of electricity, gas, and water. The DJUA is known for its focus on stable, high-dividend-paying companies in the utility sector.

4. Dow Jones Composite Average (DJCA)

The DJCA combines the DJIA, DJTA, and DJUA, encompassing 65 companies. It provides a broader view of the market by including industrials, transportation, and utilities.

5. Dow Jones U.S. Total Stock Market Index

This index represents the entire U.S. stock market, including large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap companies. It offers a comprehensive benchmark for the performance of U.S. equities.

6. Dow Jones Sustainability Indices (DJSI)

Launched in 1999, the DJSI track the performance of companies leading in sustainability practices. They evaluate companies based on economic, environmental, and social criteria.

7. Dow Jones Global Titans 50 Index

This index includes 50 of the largest and most liquid blue-chip companies worldwide. It provides insights into the performance of global giants across various industries.

8. Dow Jones BRIC 50 Index

The DJ BRIC 50 Index tracks 50 leading companies from Brazil, Russia, India, and China, offering a gauge of economic performance and growth prospects in these emerging markets.

9. Dow Jones Select Dividend Index

This index measures the performance of leading dividend-paying stocks in the U.S. It includes companies with a strong history of dividend payouts.

10. Dow Jones U.S. Large-Cap Total Stock Market Index

This index focuses on large-cap U.S. stocks, providing a benchmark for the performance of the largest companies by market capitalization in the United States.

11. Dow Jones U.S. Small-Cap Total Stock Market Index

Tracking the performance of small-cap stocks in the U.S., this index offers insights into the segment of the market comprising smaller companies.

12. Dow Jones Islamic Market Index

This index includes companies that comply with Islamic Shariah law, offering a benchmark for investors seeking Shariah-compliant investment opportunities.

13. Dow Jones Commodity Index

This index tracks futures contracts on physical commodities, including energy, metals, and agriculture. It provides a measure of commodity market performance.

14. Dow Jones Global Real Estate Index

The DJ Global Real Estate Index tracks the performance of publicly traded real estate companies and REITs worldwide, providing insights into the global real estate market.

15. Dow Jones U.S. Total Stock Market Float Adjusted Index

This index represents the total stock market of the U.S. on a float-adjusted basis, which means it only considers shares available for public trading.

In Summary

These top 15 Dow Jones indexes managed by S&P Dow Jones Indices play a crucial role in the financial markets by providing benchmarks that represent various sectors, regions, and investment strategies. They help investors, analysts, and policymakers understand market trends, manage investments, and make informed decisions.
